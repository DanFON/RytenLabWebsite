Research Interests

  • Investigating the impact of genetic variation on gene expression and splicing within human tissues.
  • Identifying novel variants that relate to the pathogenesis of neurological disease.
  • Developing bioinformatics analysis pipelines that combine functional transcriptomic information with DNA-sequencing to improve the rate of genetic diagnosis.

    • Biography

    I completed my BSc Biomedical science in 2015 and went on to undertake a MSc Neuroscience degree, during which I studied the role of mitochondrial dysfunction in the pathogenesis of neurodegeneration in Xeroderma Pigmentosum patients. My first introduction to bioinformatics came following year whilst I was working as a research technician, where I investigated the effect of genetic variation on the age of onset of dementia and cognition within Down syndrome patients. I started my PhD in the Ryten lab in 2017 and my project currently involves using transcriptomic data increase the diagnostic yield from genetic testing.
